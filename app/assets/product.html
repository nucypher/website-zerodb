ZeroDB Secure Premise

End-to-End on premise big data power delivered with a new encryption layer

The world’s first End-to-End encryption appliance for on-premise Hadoop installations.

90% of big data deployments reside on premise. Enterprise big data science teams now come from multiple stakeholders in the enterprise and relevant business partners. The need to secure on premise big data installations is important now more than ever as more power comes to the on premise big data center.

Improved Key Management

No key cache or caching proxy of data encryption keys are required in the Key Management System. Customers get additional power and performance from ZeroDB’s proxy encryption system for key management.

On Premise Encryption Appliance

Software appliance designed to seamlessly work alongside any hadoop installations to deliver client-side controlled encryption keys for each hadoop compute process. No code rewrites necessary!

End-to-End Compliance Support

Compliance and regulatory requirements, whether prescriptive or directive require ongoing enforcement of data protection at all points in the big data workflow. End-to-End encryption enables enterprise to deliver on privacy and compliance requirements through the entire system as a complete solution.

Infrastructure Secured.
on-premise or in the cloud.

ZeroDB End-to-End Secure Big Data is the world’s first End-to-End encryption appliance for Hadoop installations. The installation knows nothing about the data it is storing, greatly reducing the risk of data breach. Query logic is performed client-side against encrypted data on a remote server.

Even if the data is hacked, an attacker will not be able to compromise the underlying data.

Compliant Cloud.

ZeroDB Secure Big Data enables you to offload on-premise big data and infrastructure to the cloud. Enjoy the cost and performance benefits of the cloud without comprimising on security or usability. Your can even securely share your data with third-parties using proxy re-encryption technology.

Hadoop reached 1.0 in 2011. In the five years since, over 80% of deployments in the enterprise have been on-premise. This is because the native security controls in Hadoop are not sufficiently robust to meet very demanding enterprise security requirements.
